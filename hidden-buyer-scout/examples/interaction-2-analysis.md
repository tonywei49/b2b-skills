# Buyer Direction Analysis Example

Search target:
- Product: children's balls
- Market: UAE
- Goal: identify hidden buyers beyond sports distributors

## Strong Logic Segments

### 1. Hotels and resorts with kids clubs
- Usage scenario: balls used in kids clubs, family recreation zones, poolside activity sessions, holiday event programming
- Buying logic: these venues run repeated family-facing programs and often need low-cost, replaceable activity equipment
- Likely buyer role: kids club manager, recreation manager, procurement manager
- Purchase frequency: recurring or seasonal
- Why this is strong: direct operational use, repeated guest turnover, and visible activity programming

### 2. Schools and education groups
- Usage scenario: PE classes, early childhood movement activities, indoor play sessions, after-school programs
- Buying logic: schools often buy in batches for classes, age groups, or term-based activity planning
- Likely buyer role: PE coordinator, school operations manager, procurement lead
- Purchase frequency: recurring or term-based
- Why this is strong: the product directly fits structured group activity use

## Non-Traditional But High-Potential Segments

### 3. Family entertainment centers
- Usage scenario: soft play zones, seasonal events, toddler activity corners, sensory play areas
- Buying logic: these operators need durable, replaceable play items and may buy mixed equipment bundles
- Likely buyer role: operations manager, venue manager, purchasing lead
- Purchase frequency: recurring
- Why this is promising: not a traditional sports buyer, but still highly scenario-compatible

### 4. Shopping malls with event programming or children's zones
- Usage scenario: holiday activations, kids workshops, mall events, family attraction zones
- Buying logic: activity materials are often sourced repeatedly through mall event teams or external activation agencies
- Likely buyer role: mall marketing manager, events lead, outsourced event agency buyer
- Purchase frequency: seasonal or campaign-based
- Why this is promising: product may appear as event material rather than sports equipment

## Experimental Segments

### 5. Event and promotional agencies
- Usage scenario: branded giveaways, family-day kits, experiential campaigns, pop-up activation kits
- Buying logic: agencies may buy in bulk for specific brand campaigns or corporate events
- Likely buyer role: account manager, sourcing manager, event production lead
- Purchase frequency: project-based
- Why this is experimental: demand can be real, but it depends heavily on campaign style and client briefs

### 6. Rehabilitation and pediatric therapy centers
- Usage scenario: gross motor training, sensory play, pediatric therapy exercises
- Buying logic: small but specialized recurring demand, especially when products are used across multiple therapy sessions
- Likely buyer role: clinic manager, therapy program director, procurement staff
- Purchase frequency: recurring but lower volume
- Why this is experimental: strong logic for some products, but not all ball types fit clinical use

## Recommended Search Focus

Recommended priority:
1. hotels and resorts
2. schools and education groups
3. family entertainment centers

Secondary expansion:
4. malls with children programming
5. event agencies
6. therapy centers
